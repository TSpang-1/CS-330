# CS-330

How do I approach designing software?

When I design software, I usually start by thinking about what I want my final result to look like and then break it down into smaller parts. For this project, I pictured a simple outdoor park scene and then focused on building each object one at a time, like the bench, tree, and then the lamp post. This project helped me to get better at thinking ahead before coding, especially in terms of placement, scale, and how objects fit together in a scene.

What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?

I learned how to think more visually when I am designing something. Instead of just focusing on the code, I also had to consider things like the object proportions, lighting, and how everything looks together in a 3D space. I also learned more about organizing my scene and using reusable functions, which made my code much cleaner and easier to manage.

What design process did you follow for your project work?

I followed a step-by-step process where I first created the basic objects, then added in the textures, and finally I worked on the lighting and camera controls. I didn’t try to do everything all at one time. Breaking it into smaller steps made my project feel much more manageable and helped me to stay on track.

How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?

The biggest thing I would carry forward is breaking large problems down into smaller pieces. That made a huge difference in staying focused and not getting overwhelmed by evrything. I would also continue planning things out before coding, especially for more complex projects.

How do I approach developing programs?

When developing programs, I usually build things slowly and test it as I go. I don’t expect everything to work right away, so I try to fix small issues before moving on. This helps me to understand what is going wrong instead of getting stuck later on.

What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?

One big strategy I used was reusing my code for multiple objects instead of just rewriting everything. I also got more comfortable adjusting values like the position, scale, and lighting to improve how my scene looked. I also paid more attention to debugging, especially when things didn’t render correctly.

How did iteration factor into your development?

Iteration was a big part of this project. I constantly made small changes, tested them, and then had to adjust them again. For example, I changed object sizes, the lighting settings, and textures multiple times until the scene finally looked the way I wanted it to.

How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones?

At the beginning, I was mostly just trying to get things to work right. By the end, I was more focused on improving the look, organization, and overall quality of my code. I also became more confident in making changes and experimenting without worrying about acciedentally breaking everything.

How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?

Computer science gives me the skills to solve problems and build things on my own. It also opens up career opportunities that are more stable and flexible. This is important for me because I want to move into a better career path and become more independent.

How do computational graphics and visualizations help in your future educational pathway?

This project helped me understand how visual systems work, which is useful for learning more advanced topics later on. It also made programming feel more interactive and engaging. I think it will help me stay more interested in my future courses and programming projects.

How do computational graphics and visualizations help in your future professional pathway?

Even if I don’t go directly into graphics, the skills I learned in this course like problem-solving, debugging, and building structured programs are useful in a lot of other tech roles. It also gave me experience creating something visual that I can show in my portfolio, which could help when applying for jobs.
